Continuity

In "White Space is Not Your Enemy", chapter 5, written by Hagen & Golombisky, it states, “Our minds will continue a pattern beyond its ending points. Further, our eyes will follow the direction of a line… Applying this concept can add a sense of direction and movement to your layout.”

Continuity is only possible because our minds, if our brains could not follow a direction of a line past the lines end, continuity would be pointless. But since our minds do this, it is possible to form “paths” across the screen and the viewers eye will follow it, which can be done by placing a row of icons to click on, on a webpage. An invisible line will end up guiding the viewers eye from the icon on the far left of the page, to the far right of the page.

Continuity is pictured above in a Coca-Cola label. The logo on the label is what has continuity because the viewers eye follows the C in Coca to the beginning of Cola. Even though the bottom of the C does not connect the words, our minds can visualize where the line is going.
