Balance

Hagen & Golombisky state in "White Space is Not Your Enemy" in chapter 5, “In design, we think of balance in terms of visual weight. You want your designs and layouts to be visually balanced… There are three kinds of visual balance: radial, symmetrical and asymmetrical. Radial balance refers to circular designs… to achieve symmetrical balance, each side of a bisected deign must be a mirror image of the other in terms of visual weight… Asymmetry, then, reveals two unequal sides if bisected.”

Balance is a very important aspect of a website because it determines if your layout looks visually appealing. If the layout is unbalanced, viewers might find your website overwhelming or distracting. Positive and negative space, and dark and light values are elements of a website that you have to be careful of when dealing with balance. For example, positive space carries more weight than negative space therefore you must have an equal amount of empty space as you do full.
