Movement

According to chapter 5 of "White Space is Not Your Enemy", by Hagen & Golombisky, “The flow of lines can move the eye across the page or screen. Lines then, can create movement, and different kinds of linear movement tend to communicate different kinds of symbolic messages.” Hagen & Golombisky describe movement as having to do with lines, and the flow of those lines across the page or in a picture.

The use of different types of lines show different types of movement. Different types of lines used in websites include horizontal, vertical, diagonal, and curved lines. These lines communicate different types of messages to the viewer, for example a vertical line can represent growth or a decline, while a curving line can be leading your eye towards something important or represent distance.
