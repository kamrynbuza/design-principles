Unity

According to Hagen & Golombisky in "White Space is Not Your Enemy", chapter 5, “Unity means that all of the parts of the design work together, and everything looks like it belongs together… Unity refers to oneness, that the result is one cohesive design or visual message.”

Without unity, a website looks unfinished. Focal point, contrast, balance, movement, and rhythm all compliment each other to create the finished feel of unity. Another easy way to achieve unity when designing the content for a website is to choose a theme, and make all the principles of design conform to that theme.

The Virginia Tech flag is a perfect real world example of unity. The university flag is unified with the colors of Chicago maroon and burnt orange, and with the use of similar font. Contrast, rhythm and movement are also created with lines and colors to create a feeling of unity.
