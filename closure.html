Closure

As stated in chapter 5 of "White Space is Not Your Enemy", by Hagen & Golombisky, we use closure to “Mentally fill in the gaps in order to complete a perceived shape… The idea of designing with only a part but having your viewer perceive the whole opens up interesting compositional opportunities, including the interplay of positive and negative space.”

Leaving gaps in words or shapes allows the viewers mind to finish the shape or word. The point of doing this is to make icons or logos more interesting. Aspects like closure can help draw people to your website because it is more appealing to look at. Another way to achieve closure besides leaving gaps, is by cropping parts of images or icons by placing them in the corners of your website. Just like leaving gaps, the viewer still has to mentally finish the image.

In this image you see a collection of lipglosses. The main lipgloss to focus on is in the middle with the brand name Dose. This brand logo uses the law of closure because it allows your mind to finish the letters D and E. The image shows that closure can be achieved in extremely simple ways, and does not always have to be complicated.
