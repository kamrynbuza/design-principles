Proximity

Chapter 5 of "White Space is Not Your Enemy", by Hagen & Golombisky, explains proximity as “Objects that are close together as belonging to the same group. A related law, the law of Common Fate, says that we perceive objects moving in the same direction as part of the same group… Placing elements in proximity to one another goes back to clumping.”

Proximity is one of the four Gestalt laws. Sectioning or grouping items on a website is the easiest way to follow Gestalt’s law of proximity. Grouping objects together avoids the page from being cluttered or overwhelming to look at. However, while grouping items together on a website helps the viewer understand that they are related, it is important to avoid putting too many items in one section.

Makeup palettes are a simple example of proximity in the real world. The eyeshadows inside the palette are placed together because of the similar colors which all work well with each other. Just like you would place eyeshadows in a palette together that compliment each other, you would place images or icons together on a website that compliment each other.
