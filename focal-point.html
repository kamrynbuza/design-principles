Focal Point

According to "White Space is Not Your Enemy" by Hagen & Golombisky in chapter 5, “The focal point is the center of attention in the design or layout… Rule No.1 about focal points: Have one. Without a focal point, the viewer doesn’t know where to look first… Rule No. 2 about focal points: Limit one per screen or page, or story or ad. Without a focal point, the eye wanders aimlessly around the layout.”

A focal point is one of the most important aspects of design in a website because it draws the viewer in and keeps their attention. The focal point should catch the viewers eye, creating contrast with colors, lines, values, or shapes can help achieve this. Another way to do this is by placing an object in front of other objects in a photograph.

The focal point in the picture above is the sunflower on the right. That sunflower is the focal point not only because it is in front of all the other flowers, but also because it is the only sunflower being focused on. The way this picture was taken, helps guide the viewers eye to what the photographer wants the viewer to focus on.
