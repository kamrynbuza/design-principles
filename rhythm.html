Rhythm

In "White Space is Not Your Enemy", chapter 5, Hagen & Golombisky explain that, “Rhythmic movement has to do with repeating items strategically… Repeating pictures of moons throughout the layout creates a kind of rhythm. Repeating a color such as the yellow of the moon photo also can create rhythmic movement.”

When creating a pattern, whether it is irregular or regular, it involves repeating and some kind of strategic movement. A rhythm, or pattern, helps guide the viewers eye across the page and provides a sense of unity to the website. Another way of adding rhythm to the page besides color and photos, is by using columns to separate text.

Rhythm is applied in this photo of daily planner because of the repeated use of color and shape. The planner uses an irregular pattern of cactus shapes, but the same shapes are still being repeated which creates rhythm. The pages of the planner also match the colors of the cover, which creates unity and rhythm throughout the whole planner.
