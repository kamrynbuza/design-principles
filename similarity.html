Similarity

Similarity, as described in chapter 5 of "White Space is Not Your Enemy", by Hagen & Golombisky, is as simple as, “Like goes with like.” For example, squares would be grouped with squares and circles would be grouped with circles. Along with shapes, you can also group things based on colors “To create order and organization.”

Organization is extremely important when designing a webpage, and applying the law of similarity helps accomplish it. Applying similarity to a website is as simple as making sure the navigation links are all in the same section and have the same font. You also achieve similarity by having similarly designed or styled headings and subheadings. Similarity makes a website easy to use, because it will be easy to read.

Similarity is applied to these everyday beauty items by the use of similar fonts and colors. It is especially shown in the bottle of lotion due to the headings, subheadings, and body text being styled similarly, but still different enough so that the buyer can see a difference. Similarity is involved in all sorts of common household items, but it is normal to not notice until you understand the law of similarity.
